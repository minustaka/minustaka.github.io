Well, Quantum Superposition would be, a thing being on two states at the same time until measured. Pretty weird... but, it's what Quantum Superposition is. To better explain, here is an example: A cat is in a box with a bomb. The bomb is connected to a particle. If the particle's spin is downward, the bomb won't explode, but if its spin is upwards, it would explode. And, in Quantum Mechanics, there is this thing called Superposition. As I mentioned earlier, its a particle being in two state at the same time until measured. So, we won't know if the bomb exploded or not unless we take a look at the insides of the box. Now, since we don't know if the bomb had exploded or not, it is on a Superposition. Exploded and not, at the same time. And since the bomb exploded and not at the same time, the cat would also be alive and not at the same time. It being dead and not at the same time would mean it is on a Superposition. Get it? There is this theory called Many-Worlds Interpretation. This theory suggests that, everytime a superposition is broken, the universe splits itself. Let's assume we measured the spin of an electron. And then let's assume the spin of the electron is upwards, the universe will split itself to make another universe where the spin of the electron is downwards. Hence, there is a parallel universe where the spin of that electron is downwards, opposite of this universe, where its spin is upwards.
